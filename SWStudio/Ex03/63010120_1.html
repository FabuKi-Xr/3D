What is chickpea?

Chickpeas are a type of legume in the same family as kidney beans and peanuts. They are a good source of protein, fiber, and several vitamins and minerals. Chickpeas are also high in antioxidants, which may help protect against heart disease and cancer. Chickpeas are a popular ingredient in many dishes, including hummus, falafel, and curries. They are also used to make flour, which can be used to make bread and pasta. Chickpeas are also available in canned form, which is convenient for cooking.

Nutrition

Chickpeas boast an impressive nutritional profile.They contain a moderate number of calories, providing 269 per cup (164 grams). Approximately 67% of these calories come from carbs, while the rest comes from protein and fat (1Trusted Source).Chickpeas also provide a variety of vitamins and minerals, as well as a decent amount of fiber and protein. A 1-cup (164-gram) serving of cooked chickpeas offers

  • Calories: 269
  • Protein: 14.5 grams
  • Fat: 4 grams
  • Carbs: 45 grams
  • Fiber: 12.5 grams
  • Manganese: 74% of the Daily Value (DV)
  • Folate (vitamin B9): 71% of the DV
  • Copper: 64% of the DV
  • Iron: 26% of the DV
  • Zinc: 23% of the DV
  • Phosphorus: 22% of the DV
  • Magnesium: 19% of the DV
  • Thiamine: 16% of the DV
  • Vitamin B6: 13% of the DV
  • Selenium: 11% of the DV
  • Potassium: 10% of the DV
